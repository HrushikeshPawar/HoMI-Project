Āryabhaṭīya System

Āryabhaṭa devised this system and used it in his famous Āryabhaṭīya. No other Indian astronomer or mathematician seems to have employed this system given its complexity. Here, both consonants and vowels represent numbers. While consonants k to m and y to h depict numbers from 1 to 25 and 30 to 100 respectively, vowels depict the decimal values 100 to 1016. Our application decodes and encodes this system from string to number and vice versa respectively. For more details, go to Āryabhaṭa numeration

Examples for the decoder:
  • ख्युघृ - Devnagari
  • चयगियिङुशुछृलृ - Devnagari
  • ​​buPinaca - SLP1
  • maKi - SLP1
  • BaKi - SLP1
